# office-hours — pressure-test an idea before it becomes a plan

Run at IDEATE, before any plan. Get the idea sharp (or kill it) cheaply.

## Trigger
- "I have an idea", "is this worth building", "help me think this through", "brainstorm this".

## Procedure

### Phase 1 — Context
Understand what they're proposing, for whom, and why now — in their words. Don't critique yet.

### Phase 2 — Pick a mode
- **Startup mode (YC diagnostic)** — for things meant to get users/revenue. Ask the six forcing
  questions, one at a time, demanding specifics:
  1. **Demand reality** — who *desperately* wants this, and how do you know (not "people would like it")?
  2. **Status quo** — what do they use today, and why is it painful enough to switch?
  3. **Desperate specificity** — name the actual first user. One real person/company.
  4. **Narrowest wedge** — the smallest thing that's still valuable; what do you cut?
  5. **Observation** — what have you seen/heard that others missed (the unfair insight)?
  6. **Future-fit** — why does this get bigger over time, not commoditized away?
- **Builder mode (design partner)** — for side projects / learning / hackathons / OSS. Less
  interrogation, more design-thinking: explore the space, the fun, the constraints, what you'll learn.

### Phase 3 — Challenge the premise
Surface the load-bearing assumption and stress it. Offer a sharper framing or a narrower wedge.
Be honest if the idea isn't worth building yet — that's a valid, valuable outcome.

### Phase 4 — Capture
Save a short design doc (the idea, the answers, the wedge, the verdict) so it can feed a PRD
(`to-prd`) or a CEO-lens review (`plan-ceo-review`).

## Operating Rules — MANDATORY
Obey `.helmsman/harness/operating-rules.md`. One forcing question at a time; demand specifics,
reject vague answers. "Not worth building yet" is an honest, allowed conclusion — never
manufacture enthusiasm.

## Evidence (what "done" must show)
The answers to the six questions (startup) or the explored design space (builder), the named
wedge, and the saved design doc with a clear verdict.
